# Validation Playbook

## Goal

Validate whether a Korea-focused `market brief home` is something people will revisit and eventually pay for.

## What We Need To Learn

### Problem Fit

- do target users already stitch together multiple sources every day?
- is there real frustration with current market-checking workflows?
- does one personalized screen feel materially better than existing portals?

### Habit Fit

- would users open this every morning or after work?
- is the `brief + cards` format more useful than a normal finance page?

### Payment Fit

- would users pay for a cleaner workflow, deeper briefs, or premium cards?
- is the natural model subscription, sponsor-supported, or mostly free?

## Test 1: Problem Interviews

### Target

- 10 Korea-based retail investors
- mixed profiles:
- domestic equity focused
- Korea + US mixed
- macro / FX / commodity watchers

### Interview Goals

- understand current market-checking routine
- understand what users patch together manually
- see whether this dashboard solves a real habit problem

### Core Questions

1. 아침이나 장 시작 전 시장을 어떻게 확인하나요?
2. 한국장, 미국장, 환율, 금리, 원자재를 어디서 각각 보나요?
3. 지금 시장 확인 과정에서 가장 번거로운 점은 뭔가요?
4. 한 화면에서 시장을 요약해서 보여주는 홈화면이 있으면 매일 열 것 같나요?
5. 단순 시세보다 `오늘 분위기`와 `맥락`이 같이 보이는 게 얼마나 중요한가요?
6. 지금 쓰는 서비스보다 이게 더 좋아지려면 꼭 필요한 건 무엇인가요?
7. 광고가 있는 무료 버전과 광고 없는 유료 버전 중 어느 쪽이 더 자연스럽나요?
8. 어떤 기능이면 월 구독을 고려할 수 있나요?

### Good Signal

- users already use multiple tabs, apps, or pages every day
- users say they want a cleaner daily market workflow
- users react positively to a personalized dashboard metaphor

### Bad Signal

- users only care about execution speed or trading depth
- users say existing portals are already enough
- users do not see daily revisit value

## Test 2: Landing Page

### Goal

- test which framing attracts more interest before deeper product work

### Positioning Variant A

- headline: `내가 매일 여는 시장 브리핑 홈`
- message: see Korea, US, FX, rates, commodities, and crypto in one organized screen

### Positioning Variant B

- headline: `내 손으로 조립하는 시장 홈화면`
- message: build your own market dashboard with cards that match how you actually watch markets

### Core CTA

- `대기자 명단 참여`
- `미리 보기 받기`
- `관심 있는 기능 남기기`

### What To Measure

- click-through rate
- waitlist conversion rate
- which headline gets stronger interest
- which feature requests appear most often

## Test 3: Premium Fake Door

### Goal

- test willingness to pay before building premium features

### Premium Concepts To Test

- deeper morning / close brief
- ad-free dashboard
- premium cards
- advanced personal layouts

### Fake Door CTA Examples

- `프리미엄 브리프 신청`
- `광고 없는 홈화면 대기하기`
- `고급 카드 먼저 써보기`

### What To Measure

- click rate on premium CTAs
- % of interested users
- what users expect to be paid vs free

## Early Revenue Hypotheses

### Most Plausible

- subscription for better workflow and premium cards
- sponsor card for finance-adjacent brands
- ad-free tier

### Less Plausible Early

- broad display ads only
- data-heavy real-time quote subscription
- B2B pivot before B2C demand exists

## Go / No-Go Thresholds

## Go

- at least 6 out of 10 interviews show strong workflow pain
- at least one positioning variant gets clear positive response
- premium fake door gets meaningful curiosity from early users

## Caution

- users like the idea but do not see daily habit value
- users want raw speed more than organized context
- premium interest is vague or weak

## No-Go / Reframe

- users do not see a problem worth solving
- the product is consistently described as redundant with existing portals
- there is no meaningful revisit or payment signal

## Immediate Deliverables

1. interview notes sheet
2. one simple landing page
3. premium fake-door CTA copy
4. summary of signals after 10 interviews
